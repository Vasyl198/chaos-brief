# Chaos Brief — first paid review kit

Use this only after a person has submitted a real request. Do not publish bank details; send EUR SEPA details privately after the scope is confirmed.

## 1. Scope confirmation

> Hi [name] — thanks for the context. I can review this decision as a focused Chaos Brief.
>
> **What you will receive within 24 hours:**
> - the smallest first-release boundary to test;
> - assumptions and evidence gaps that could change the decision;
> - change triggers, acceptance criteria, and a cheaper fallback;
> - one clarification round.
>
> **Scope:** [one sentence describing the decision]
>
> **Price:** €15 (intro price for one focused decision review).
>
> If this matches what you need, reply “confirmed” and I will send private EUR SEPA payment details. Work starts when the payment arrives.

## 2. Private payment request

> Thanks — the scope is confirmed.
>
> **Amount:** €15 EUR
> **Method:** SEPA bank transfer
> **Recipient:** [recipient name exactly as shown by the bank]
> **IBAN:** [paste privately from the bank app]
> **BIC:** [paste privately from the bank app, if requested]
> **Payment reference:** Chaos Brief review — [client/project]
>
> Please send only EUR. SEPA transfers usually take 1–5 business days. Send a confirmation when it is complete; I will confirm when it arrives and start the 24-hour review window.

## 3. Payment received

> Payment received — thank you. The review window has started now.
>
> I will send the completed Chaos Brief by [date and time, timezone]. If one fact is missing, I will ask one focused clarification rather than make it up.

## Before sending a paid deliverable

- Confirm that the decision, scope, price, and deadline match the message.
- Do not invent a fact that the client did not provide.
- Name exactly one primary flow; defer the rest explicitly.
- Make every acceptance criterion observable.
- Keep private payment details out of the deliverable and any public link.
